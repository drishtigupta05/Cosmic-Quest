Timeline of Space Exploration

1957

i) October 4- The Soviet Union launched the first satellite, Sputnik, into space.

ii) November 3- The Soviet spacecraft Sputnik 2 was launched with a dog named Laika on board. Laika did not survive the voyage.

1958

January 31- Explorer 1 was the first satellite launched by the union states whe it was sent into orbit on January 31, 1958. It was designed and built by the Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL) of the California Institute of Technology. The satellite was sent aloft from Cape Canaveral in Florida by the Jupiter C rocket that was designed, built, and launched by the Army Ballistic Missile Agency (ABMA) under the direction of Dr. Wernher Von Braun.

1961

i) April 12- Russian cosmonaut Yuri Gagarin became the first human in space.

ii) May 5- Astronaut Alan Shepard became the fisrt American in space.

iii) May 25- President Kennedy challange the country to put a man on the moon by the end of the decade.

1966

i) Feburary 3- The Russian spacecraft Luna 9 became the fisrt spacecraft to land on the moon.

ii) June 2- Surveyor 1 became the first American spacecraft to land in the moon.

1969

July 20- Neil Armstrong and "Buzz" Aldrin became the first men on the moon

1970

i) April 11- Apollo 13 was launched.

ii) September 12- The Soviet craft Luna 16 was launched and became the first automatic spacecraft to return soil samples of the moon.

iii) November 17- The Soviet automatic robot Lunokhod 1 landed on the moon with Luna 17.

iv) December 1117- The Soviet Venera 7 became the first probe to land on Venus.

1976

September- The American probe viking to discover water frost on the Martian surface.

1981

i) April 12- Columbia became the fisrt Space Shuttle to be launched.

ii) August 26- Voyager to reached Saturn and began transmitting images.

2003

i) Febuary 1- The Space Shuttle Columbia brokeup on re-entry into the earth's atmosphere.

ii) August 25- NASA launched the largest-diametre infrared telescope ever in space, The Spitzer Space Telescope.

iii) September 21- NASA's Galileo mission ended a 14 year exploration of the solar systems largest planet and it's moons with the spacecraft crasing by designed into jupiter at 108,000 mph.

2009

i) March 6- The NASA spacecraft Kepler was launched. It's mission is to search for planets outside our solar sysytem, in a distant area of Milky Way.

ii) June 18- NASA launched the LUNAR crater observation and sensing satellite, also known as LCROSS. The mission is to confirm the presence or absence of ice on the moon. On November 13, 2009, NASA scientists announce the discovery of a "significant amount" of ice in a crater near the moons south pole.

2012

i) May 22- SpaceX, a commercial space company, launched its dragon c2+ mission to resupply the international space.

ii) August 6- NASA's curiosity rover successfully landed on Mars. As large as a car, it carried an array of advanced new instruments and experiments.

2016

i) August 28- ISRO Scramjet Engine Technology Demonstrator.

ii) August 26- Voyager to reached Saturn and began transmitting images.
